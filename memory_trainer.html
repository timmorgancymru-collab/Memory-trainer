Daily Plan: New Memory + Old Memory

Two tracks, 5 minutes each, every day at 19:30. New = Cards & PAO (novel material every session). Old = Oscars & Mercury (stable facts, drilled by retrieval).

The terminology, briefly

Cognitive psychology splits long-term factual/experiential memory into two kinds: episodic memory (a specific, unrepeated event — this shuffled deck, this list of digits) and semantic memory (stable general knowledge with no particular "when" attached — who won Best Picture in 1994). Your "new" exercise trains fast encoding into episodic memory; your "old" exercise trains retrieval practice (repeated recall) against fixed semantic facts, the same mechanism spaced-repetition apps like Anki rely on.

How to run the 10 minutes (19:30 daily)

5 min — New (Cards & PAO): see the phase you're in below. Always untimed until noted otherwise; accuracy first.

5 min — Old (Oscars & Mercury): open today's category shown above in the Oscars & Mercury tab and run flashcards until the timer stops you. Missed cards reshuffle back into the deck automatically, so they'll resurface — that repetition is the point.

New-memory phases (Cards & PAO)

Phase 1 (days 1–7): My Systems → PAO Card System. Untimed lookup + flashcard quiz only. Goal: instant person+action+object recall for any card shown, no exceptions.

Phase 2 (days 8–14): Cards tab — shuffle a deck but only memorise the first 5 cards dealt, using your PAO images. Check recall. Once 3 days in a row are perfect, move to 10 cards.

Phase 3 (weeks 3–4): Grow to 15, then 20 cards, still untimed. Keep it bite-size — stop at your target count even if more of the deck is showing.

Phase 4 (month 2+): Once 20 cards feels easy untimed, switch on the clock and start working toward the full 52-card deck — this is the actual WMC Speed Cards discipline. Track your personal best.
